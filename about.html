The Citizen Participation Institute is a tax-deductible nonpartisan Illinois charity founded in 2015 by incorporators Stanley R. Zegel, Shannon M. Burns, and Burton D. Minor to aid area citizens in exercising their rights to open government.


One of our initiatives is to increase the probability of voters having actual choices they can make in nonpartisan elections. Most offices have only one candidate, but by making it easier for others to run too, voters can have a choice instead of a default.


We publish materials related to the Freedom of Information Act, the Open Meetings Act, and the Statements of Economic Interests required by the Governmental Ethics Act, including compilations of legal precedents under those Sunshine Laws. We assist citizens who are experiencing problems securing public information or encountering public meeting violations.


We publish on this website copyrighted lists we have compiled of the email addresses that citizens can use to send Freedom of Information requests to any of the 8,511 units of local government in Illinois. It is the only complete list of those local governments available. We intend to make it possible for a citizen to fill in a request form on our website, and it will automatically be emailed to the proper official.


We are publishing a directory of nonpartisan elective local offices to attract more candidates, and will allow candidates to download all of the paperwork they need to run for a local office such as mayor, city council, village president, village board, school board, fire board, park board, library board and other nonpartisan offices.


We will be publishing a free newspaper for local government officials and other interested persons that educates officials on the latest developments in Illinois open government affairs. It will include summaries of the latest court decisions and administrative interpretations of the Freedom of Information Act and the Open Meetings Act.


We depend upon volunteers and donations to operate, and invite them from foundations, businesses, and individuals.
