Challenges to Nomination Papers

With 40,000 elected offices in Illinois, in the limited time available between filing nomination papers and needing to prepare the ballot, no court or officials could possibly validate all the signatures and other requirements of nomination papers. Illinois law assumes that the other candidates (or their friends) will examine the papers of their opponents and bring to the attention of the authorities any defects discovered. It is a valuable process that weeds out candidates who cannot follow the law's simple directions in preparing their own important papers from being elected to a position where they must follow detailed directions set by law in spending taxpayer money or administering important functions to their residents.

That process is initiated by filing, within five business days after the close of the filing period, a written Objection with the office where the nomination papers were filed. It need not be in any specific form as long as it states the name and residence address of the objector, states what his interest is in filing the objection, states fully exactly what is wrong with the nomination papers, and what the objector wants the officials to do about it.

The law creates an Electoral Board with automatic members to hear the case, often of local elected officials. Those persons are not experts in Illinois election law, and they often end up making up the rules as they go along, unaware of how other Electoral Boards or Courts have ruled on points raised that were not spelled out in detail in the written law. Sadly, many times something ruled against by one Electoral Board would have been acceptable to an Electoral Board elsewhere.

To promote uniform application of the law throughout the state, in consultation with leading election attorneys and experts the Citizen Participation Institute has curated two publications that establish a procedure that assures a party without an attorney still gets a fair hearing, and that provide pre-determined rulings for various situations.
