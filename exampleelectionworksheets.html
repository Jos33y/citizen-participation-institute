The Competitiveness Spreadsheet

There is one of these spreadsheets for each of the eight counties for the local elections of 2013 and 2015.

All the possible elective offices are listed, by public body. Using data available on line, you will enter the number of seats available then, and the number of candidates for those seats. The spreadsheet will calculate the amount of choice a voter had in that race.

This information is important to establish the base line of how easy it is for a candidate to win if he runs. After we do our educational outreach to get more people interested in running, we will be able to see if were successful in increasing voter choice in various races.

The Electoral History Spreadsheet

We will need one of these spreadsheets for each of the 1,300 public bodies expected to have candidates on the April 2017 ballot.

The bottom portion of the spreadsheet receives data you input; the top portion is all calculated automatically.

The bottom portion shows, for each two-year election cycle back to 2003, how many votes were cast, and who won each seat on the ballot. Because terms are staggered for some offices, some columns span different rows. (The figures between the quantity of votes cast, and the names, are automatically calculated based upon the votes cast.)

This worksheet calculates whose terms are due to expire (something the public bodies themselves sometimes lose track of). Information from each worksheet will be the used to build the directory of available seats up for election that we will publish in hopes of attracting more persons to become candidates.
